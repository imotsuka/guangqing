Research Experience

  1. Science Talent Program

    Ministry of Education of the PRC & China Association for Science and Technology (CAST)

    10/2017 — 4/2019

    Under the guidance of Prof. Hexiang Deng, I conducted multiple syntheses of transition metal-based MOF materials aimed at discovering improved nitrogen-absorbing agents. These MOFs underwent comprehensive characterization utilizing techniques such as XRD and various spectroscopic methods. The conclusive findings were presented at the 2018 Science Talent Program Summit hosted by University of Science and Technology of China(USTC), Hefei, Anhui, PRC.
    ↓See Prof. Deng's webpage here Deng's Group

  2. Undergraduate Chemistry Laboratory Innovation Design Competition

    Peking University

    03/2022 — 07/2022

    Guided by the proficient mentorship of instructors at the Experimental Chemical Center, two fellow undergraduate students and I meticulously examined a spectrum of research reports pertaining to rapid photoswitching compounds. Subsequently, we identified a binuclear Zn(II) complex from the publication Dalton Trans., 2014, 43, 6365, as the most suitable candidate for a spectrometer-based half-life measurement laboratory. We refined the synthesis conditions for the azophenol-containing salen-type ligand, moreover, we successfully engineered a rudimentary photoexcitation employing Lego components and optical instruments. finally we managed to adapt the above two parts for integration into an undergraduate laboratory curriculum. Our innovative design garnered recognition, securing the Regional First Prize in the North China Division and achieving the 4th position.
    ↓See our final paper here (in Chinese) Final Paper

  3. Undergraduate Research

    Peking University

    09/2021 — Current

    Under the supervision of Prof. Junrong Zheng, I successfully developed a fluorescence-based HPLC analytical instrument designed for the detection of food additives. Leveraging commercially accessible chromatographic columns and mobile phase pumps, I design the spacial arragement of the instrument and applied the principle of quantum avalanche to significantly amplify the fluorescence signal with ultrasensitive SiPM. This work led to achieving an exceptional detection sensitivity, with the lowest attainable limit of 0.01 times the current standard concentration.
    ↓See Prof. Zheng's webpage here Zheng's Group

  4. Summer Internship

    The Univeristy of Chicago

    06/2023 — 09/2023

    Guided by the mentorship of Professor Philippe Guyot-Sionnest, I conducted an in-depth investigation into two highly promising materials: AgAuSe and CuInSe2. These materials exhibit commendable photoluminescence properties and conduction bands, suggesting their potential for favorable intraband transitions. Such transitions hold the potential for precise adjustments in near-infrared absorption. Through meticulous refinement of the synthesis process, coupled with the utilization of photoluminescence excitation spectroscopy and the quantum dots n-doping strategy, I successfully discerned the corresponding intraband absorption phenomenon within CuInSe2 and obatain the corresponding kinetic data for the relaxation process.
    ↓See Prof. Guyot-Sionnest's webpage here Guyot-Sionnest's Group
